abstract: We study the task of lifting arbitrary quantum states and channels to purifications and Stinespring dilations, respectively, in both the probabilistic exact and deterministic approximate settings. We formalize this task through a general framework of quantum purification machines that, given a finite number of copies or uses of a black-box input, aim to output a corresponding purification or Stinespring dilation. In the probabilistic exact setting, we show that universality is not necessary to rule out such transformations. In the approximate setting, we derive analytical expressions for several physically motivated strategies together with a general upper bound that is tight in a specific regime.
