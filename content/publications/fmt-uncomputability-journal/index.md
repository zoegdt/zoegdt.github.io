abstract: This journal article studies the feasibility of computing quantum gate-circuit emulation and concatenation on digital hardware. It proves that both tasks are uncomputable under a rigorous theory of computability, including when the admissible inputs are restricted to one-parameter families of fixed gate sets. The paper argues that some ideas in quantum-computing theory may need to be reformulated to become practically implementable.
