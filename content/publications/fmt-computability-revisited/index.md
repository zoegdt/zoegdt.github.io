abstract: This article revisits quantum compiler functions that map unitary matrices to gate-circuit approximations and analyzes their realizability by algorithms. It argues that no quantum compiler function can satisfy Banach-Mazur computability, and therefore that there is no Turing machine computing such functions in the relevant sense. The paper further discusses proposed methods of quantum compiling in light of this impossibility result.
